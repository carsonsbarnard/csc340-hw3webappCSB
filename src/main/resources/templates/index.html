So, what makes the cut?

I have always been an avid lover of hip-hop, however I have some RnB albums I'd love to include as well. More thought-provoking and introspective music is really what speaks to me more. Music should always tell a story in some way, whether it's in jest or not. In that way it can be powerful, so what falls into that category for me determined what I selected.

To talk about a couple of these in detail, I'll just start at the top. Kendrick Lamar has always been an icon of lyricism and every album has become a hot topic at the time of its release. Arguably the GOAT.

With King's Disease, Nas returns to his roots of soulful beats and tracks with heaps of storytelling. No other artist can paint a picture with words the way Mr. Nasir Jones can, and through King's Disease I-III he shows that skill very evidently.

Frank Ocean, whilst having a limited discography, has some of the most good-feeling music I can find. The vibes I get listening to tracks like Lost, Ivy, Pink + White. Hopefully we get to see more soon!

2000 from Joey Badass, as well as 1999, has some insanely "classic" songs that I can recall from heart. Songs like Show Me, Survivor's Guilt, really bring out some deep feelings. Great stuff.

Mace Floyd is a local hip-hop artist out of UNCG's very own PopTech program, and has made waves in the community since. Through tracks like Courtside (featuring Jake Andrew), and his very own solo album "I Wish My Brother Harold Was Here", Mace is on track to put Greensboro on the map.

That's just my thoughts on a couple, thanks for stopping by the webpage! Make sure to check out the other child pages as well!
